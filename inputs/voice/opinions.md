## On the SEO industry

- **Most SEO retainers are 60% meetings and 40% work.** If your last
  agency had more strategy calls than rankings to show for them, that's
  why. The honest version of an SEO retainer is: 70% execution, 20%
  measurement, 10% reporting. Anything else is busywork billing.

- **The "blog 4 times a month" retainer is the most wasted spend in
  Australian SEO.** Most blog content ranks for nothing and gets read
  by no one. Two well-targeted 1,200-word service pages beat 12
  generic blog posts, every time.

- **Agencies that charge $5K/mo with no reporting and no keyword list
  are a tell.** If your agency won't tell you what they're targeting,
  fire them. Today.

- **Six-month exclusivity contracts are a red flag.** They exist to
  protect agencies from clients who notice nothing is happening. We
  cap at six months. Renew month-to-month after.

## On what tradies should actually do

- **Local SEO beats national SEO for 90% of trades.** If your service
  area is one council region, stop trying to rank for "plumber Sydney"
  and start ranking for "plumber Belrose", "plumber Frenchs Forest",
  "plumber Forestville". Three suburb pages will out-earn one generic
  Sydney page every time.

- **GBP optimisation is the cheapest highest-ROI move in local SEO.**
  Most tradies haven't claimed their service area. Haven't added their
  services. Haven't uploaded photos. Doing those three things — for
  free, in an afternoon — moves more rankings than $2,000 of blog content.

- **Reviews matter more than backlinks for local search.** If you have
  to choose between getting 20 more 5-star reviews and 5 more
  backlinks, choose reviews every time.

## On AEO and GEO

- **AEO matters now, not in 2027.** AI Overviews already appear on
  ~30% of searches. Pages optimised for them get cited by ChatGPT and
  Perplexity. Pages not optimised for them lose clicks even at #1.

- **The page that gets cited isn't always the page that ranks #1.**
  AI Overviews reward fast, clean answers in the first paragraph,
  structured data, and citable statistics. Sometimes a #6-ranked page
  wins the AI citation, which means the page sees more traffic in 2025
  than the #1 page does.

- **Schema markup got more important in 2025, not less.** Every
  "schema is dead" hot take comes from someone who hasn't actually
  tested it. Structured data tells the AI exactly what your page says.
  Without it, you're hoping.

## On pricing

- **A $1,200 audit is cheap insurance.** It buys a written report,
  prioritised recommendations, and the answer to "is SEO the right
  move for us right now". Sometimes the answer is no. That's worth
  $1,200 too.

- **Anything under $1,800/month total SEO budget can't do meaningful
  work in Sydney.** Below that floor, you're paying for someone to log
  in to GBP and shrug. Be honest: either commit at the floor or hold
  off until you can.

- **Flat pricing is harder to sell than hourly billing.** It also
  doesn't reward us for being slow. We picked flat anyway, because
  it's the only billing model where our incentive matches yours.

## On when NOT to hire us

- **Don't hire us if your site is under six months old.** SEO has
  prerequisites. A new site with zero authority will rank for nothing
  for months no matter how good the work is. Spend on Google Ads, test
  conversion, then come back.

- **Don't hire us if your conversion rate is under 1%.** More organic
  traffic to a site that doesn't convert is more wasted spend. Fix
  the site first.

- **Don't hire us if your last agency told you you'd rank in 30 days.**
  We won't promise that, and if it sounds appealing you're going to be
  disappointed in us.

- **Don't hire us if "more leads" is the only KPI you care about.**
  We care about that too. But the audit might tell us the bottleneck
  is the website, the phone answer rate, or the proposal turnaround.
  If you're not willing to fix those too, the SEO won't matter.

## On what Darnell Media will never do

- **Never charge for setup fees.** They're invented to make month one
  look profitable on a slow start. We don't do them.

- **Never lock clients into anything longer than six months.** If we
  can't keep you for six months on the work, we don't deserve to keep
  you for twelve.

- **Never publish AI-generated content without rewriting it.** Drafts
  with AI are fine. Publishing the first draft is a sin. We rewrite
  every page in our own voice — yes, even on retainer.

- **Never claim a result we can't prove.** If we ever quote a stat we
  can't back to a source, that's a fireable offence internally.

## How to use opinions in content

- **One strong opinion per page, max.** Deploy where it makes the page
  feel human instead of generic.
- **Always back an opinion with a number or a story.** "AEO matters
  now" is a bumper sticker. "AEO matters now because AI Overviews appear
  on ~30% of searches and cut top-1 clicks by half" is an opinion that
  survives challenge.
- **Tell people when NOT to hire us.** This is the single biggest voice
  tell. Generic content will never do it. Real consultants happily
  talk someone out of a $1,200 audit if a $50 hosting change will fix
  the same problem.
