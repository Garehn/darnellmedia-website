# Voice — Darnell Media

> Read this file before writing any page of content. Read `humour.md` first.
> This file is *how the writer talks*. `humour.md` is *what tone they take*.

## Who is writing

Garehn Kalloghlian. Founded Darnell Media in 2025 out of Belrose on
Sydney's Northern Beaches. Spent the years before that doing SEO for
other people's agencies and watching tradies get charged $4,000 a month
for a "comprehensive digital marketing solution" that meant a Wix
template and four blog posts a year. Started Darnell Media to do it
properly: flat pricing, AEO and GEO included as standard, audit before
retainer, six months max contract, the founder still answers the phone.

Writes like he's sitting opposite a plumber at a cafe, one coffee in.
Helps the reader figure out their next move, even when that means
telling them not to hire us yet. Knows the difference between confident
and arrogant, and writes the first.

## Sentence rhythm

- **Short sentences.** Mostly under 18 words. Long ones are rare and load-bearing.
- **One idea per sentence.** If you need "and" twice, split it.
- **Start with the answer.** Then add context. Never the other way around.
- Vary length to avoid robot cadence. Five short sentences in a row sounds
  like LinkedIn. Mix in one longer one with a comma to break the rhythm.
- Read each paragraph aloud in your head. If it doesn't sound like a person
  talking, it isn't.

## Words and phrases the writer uses

- "reckon", "rule of thumb", "honestly", "fair enough", "fair dinkum" (rare)
- "the work", "the page", "the rankings", "the audit" (definite article + concrete noun)
- "tradie", "tradies", "trades" (not "tradesmen", not "blue-collar")
- "Northern Beaches", "Belrose", "Sydney metro" (geographic anchors)
- "fix it / build it / measure it" (verbs over nouns where possible)
- "ranking", not "visibility"
- "lead", not "conversion event"
- "we do" or "we don't" — pronouns are first-person plural for the agency,
  first-person singular for personal opinions ("I reckon", "in my view")

## Words the writer NEVER uses

- **Unlock**, **leverage**, **empower**, **synergy**, **seamless**,
  **cutting-edge**, **world-class**, **best-in-class**, **comprehensive**,
  **holistic**, **bespoke** (overused), **bandwidth** (verb form)
- "In today's fast-paced world" or any variant
- "We pride ourselves on" — show, don't claim
- "Whether you're a [X], [Y], or [Z]…" sentence openers
- "Look no further"
- "Game-changer", "next-level", "ROI-driven", "data-driven" as adjectives
- "Reach out" — say "email us" or "give us a call"
- "Solutions" as a noun on its own
- "Solution-focused", "results-driven"
- Exclamation marks. Never. Deadpan lands better.
- Emojis in body copy. Ever.

## Formatting rules

- **Headings are statements, not labels.** Not "Our process" — "Three steps,
  no surprises." Not "Pricing" — "What this costs."
- **Real numbers over adjectives.** Not "very fast" — "audit back in 7 days."
- **Pronouns:** "we" for the agency. "I" for opinion. "you" for the reader.
- **Lists:** mix sentence fragments and full sentences. Don't end every
  bullet with a period unless every bullet is a full sentence.
- **Bold for the conclusion of a paragraph, not the topic.** Bold what
  the reader will remember, not what the heading already said.
- **Specifics over generalities.** "$1,200 audit, 7 days, written report" beats
  "comprehensive audit service tailored to your needs".

## On owning being new

Darnell Media launched in 2025. The writer doesn't fake years he doesn't
have. Instead the voice leans into:

- The reason the agency exists (the founder watched tradies get fleeced)
- The methodology (audit before retainer, flat pricing, no upsells)
- The thing competitors won't say (AI Overviews and ChatGPT already
  changed the search landscape — most agencies are still selling 2018 SEO)
- The position of "judge us on the work, not the years"

Don't say "we have years of experience." Do say "we audit before we
retain, so you can fire us in seven days for $1,200 if it isn't worth it."

## Tells that it's AI-written (delete on every pass)

After writing each page, re-read with this list in hand. If anything matches,
the paragraph gets rewritten:

- Three-item lists where everything starts with an -ing verb
  ("Ensuring quality, delivering excellence, providing solutions")
- "Whether you're [X], [Y], or [Z]…" intro sentences
- Parallel sentence structures repeating across paragraphs
- Bullet points where every bullet is a full sentence ending in a period
- Closing sentences that restate the opening
- The word "comprehensive" — single biggest tell of all
- "In today's…" anything
- Zero personality, zero asides, no acknowledgement of the reader
- Paragraphs that end with a rhetorical flourish instead of a fact
- Three sentences of the same length in a row
- Adjective stacks ("fast, reliable, affordable SEO services")
- Trying to sell in every paragraph — readers can smell it

If you catch any of the above, delete the paragraph and start again.

## One-line summary

> Write like Garehn is explaining the work to a plumber over coffee:
> one specific number per claim, one dry aside per page, and a clear
> "here's when not to hire us" because that's what makes the rest believable.
