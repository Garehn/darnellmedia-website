## Origin story

Garehn started Darnell Media in 2025 after watching one too many tradies
get quoted $4,000 a month for "comprehensive digital marketing" that
turned out to be a Wix template and four blog posts a year. The breaking
point was a Belrose plumber friend who paid an agency $30K over 18
months and ranked for nothing — no GBP optimisation, no on-page work,
just monthly "content strategy" calls.

Started Darnell Media to do the opposite: audit before retainer, flat
pricing, six-month maximum contract, the founder answers the phone.

**Use this story when:** writing about trust, the difference between
Darnell Media and bigger agencies, the audit-first model, the about page.

## Story 1: The Belrose plumber (composite)

<!-- TODO: replace with real client story -->

A plumber in Belrose runs a one-truck operation. Wants more emergency
hot water callouts. Old agency had him paying $2,800/mo for 18 months
with no rankings to show for it. Audit showed: GBP unverified, no
service-area set, 12 duplicate listings on AU directories, no schema
on the site, 4 paragraphs of generic copy on the homepage. Three weeks
of fixes — claim and merge GBP, schema, service-area pages for
adjacent suburbs, four 800-word service pages. Within 90 days, ranking
top 3 for "emergency plumber Belrose" and pulling 3-5 leads a week.

**The lesson:** Most local SEO problems are GBP problems plus on-page
problems. They look like ranking problems. They're not.

**Use this story when:** writing about local SEO, GBP optimisation,
when SEO works fast, the SEO for plumbers page.

## Story 2: The Eastern Suburbs electrician (composite)

<!-- TODO: replace with real client story -->

Electrician in Bondi serving the Eastern Suburbs. Site converts well
but only ranks for the business name. Asked for "lots of content".
Audit said no: site already had 14 thin blog posts ranking for nothing.
Recommendation was the opposite — consolidate 14 blog posts into 4 deep
service pages, set up GBP service-area pages for Bondi, Bondi
Junction, Waverley, Randwick. Result inside 4 months: top 5 for
"emergency electrician Bondi" and three other suburb-level queries.

**The lesson:** More pages is not the same as better pages. Sometimes
the work is to delete content, not add it.

**Use this story when:** writing about content strategy, the SEO for
electricians page, "stop publishing blog posts" opinion content,
content auditing.

## Story 3: The vet clinic that benefited from AEO (composite)

<!-- TODO: replace with real client story -->

A two-vet clinic on the North Shore. Ranks fine for "vet [suburb]" but
losing leads to AI Overviews that cited a competitor for "kennel cough
treatment" and "puppy vaccination Sydney". Solution wasn't more
backlinks. Was to restructure the existing service pages with
FAQ-style schema, direct-answer-first paragraphs, and citable
statistics. Within 8 weeks, the clinic showed up in the AI Overview
for both queries and bookings tied to those terms ~doubled.

**The lesson:** AI Overviews don't reward the highest-ranking page. They
reward the page that answers fastest and cleanest. If you're not
optimising for AEO, you're losing clicks even when you rank.

**Use this story when:** writing about AEO, AI Overviews, SEO for
vets, why traditional SEO isn't enough in 2025.

## Story 4: The retainer we walked away from (composite)

<!-- TODO: replace with real client story -->

A solar installer with three months in business and $1,200/mo to spend
asked us for a retainer. We said no. Their site was 4 weeks old, their
domain had zero backlinks, their service area covered all of Sydney
metro. SEO was the wrong move. We sent them a one-page note: spend
the $1,200 on Google Ads for one month, learn what converts, then come
back in six months with data and a budget that can do meaningful work.
They emailed back four months later with conversion data. The retainer
started in month seven and ranks were moving by month ten.

**The lesson:** SEO has prerequisites. If the conditions aren't right,
the most useful thing we can do is say so.

**Use this story when:** writing about "when not to hire us", on the
about page, on the consultant page, anywhere talking about ethics
or fit.

A landscaper paid $1,200 for an audit. Audit found the real problem
wasn't SEO at all — site was hosted on a Wix free plan that injected

## How to use these

- **Don't recite verbatim.** Adapt the bones — the suburb, the
  specific time-frame, the price — to fit the post.
- **One story per page maximum.** More than that feels like stuffing.
- **Always include one concrete detail** — a street, a price, a
  customer's situation — to make it feel real.
- **Until they're real, flag them.** Every page that uses a story
  gets `<!-- TODO: replace with real client story -->` near the
  story. Easy to grep and swap when real wins land.
- **Never invent a new one.** If none of these fits, write the post
  without a story rather than making something up.
